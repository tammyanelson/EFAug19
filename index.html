Hotter Than a Downtown Sidewalk:
Compass 2Q Reports Now Available
Hot off the presses! I'm happy to share Compass' 2Q 2019 market reports for Manhattan and Brooklyn outlining sales activity and stats across both boroughs.
 
In Manhattan, sales were up 31 percent over the first quarter and 7 percent year-over-year, with 2,380 recorded sales. Despite a slow start, 2019 is now within 1 percent of the sales pace set last year. But, we do have to take the uptick in stride. The new mansion tax that went into effect on July 1 did push activity, especially at higher price points, that might've otherwise happened later in the year. An anticipated clip to interest rates by the Fed later this month might keep sales moving into next quarter, but sellers should continue to anticipate longer lead times.
 
Things were hopping in Brooklyn last quarter as well, with sales up 9 percent compared to 2018. The bigger story was the faster pace at which BK homes found buyers. The average days on market in Brooklyn was a snappy 141 compared to Manhattan's 168 days. In fact, 41 percent of transactions in the borough happened in less than 60 days.

NYC Blacks Out on the Anniversary of a Blackout
Due to a cascade of failing ConEd cables and substations, a huge swath of Manhattan's west side lost power on July 13 in an area stretching from West 72nd Street to West 30th, from the Hudson to Fifth Avenue.  Filmmaker Joseph DiGiovanna captured the blackout from his New Jersey vantage point in this great time-lapse video
 
While it was no doubt a trying experience New Yorkers came together like only New Yorkers can. Civilians jumped in to direct traffic. Stars of Broadway shows took to the streets with impromptu performances. Some bodegas handed out free ice cream from their freezers while pizza counters offered cash-only room-temperature slices for sale. And miraculously, 25,000 fans caught inside MSG, just three songs into JLo's concert, were able to safely navigate out despite no working elevators, escalators or air-conditioning. This was a far cry from what happened in 1977. Check out this video as the city was a different place and tons of looting took place... When people say NYC is not the same as it used to be they kind of forget how violent and scary it used to be. Let's not forget the progress our city has made! 
 
This year Power was restored within a few hours. And while the recent blackout landed on the anniversary of the massive 1977 blackout (beautifully creepy), the earlier disruption plunged millions into darkness compared to 73,000 last week. #progress
